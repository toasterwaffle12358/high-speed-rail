How would high speed rail help stop climate change?
A train is the ultimate carpool.
A typical car releases 4.6 metric tons of CO2 EVERY YEAR. In contrast to this, high speed rail runs on electricity. Ideally the energy grid would be using renewable sources, but even if it isnt, high speed rail will still be more effecient due to grouping many people into one vehicle. Even though high speed rail isnt taking CO2 out of the atmosphere, it still helps prevent more going in.
How would this effect the people?
Many ways to slow climate change involve giving up something, like giving up meat, or reducing your energy usage. High speed rail would only better the lives of those using it. High speed rail could make travels faster and more enjoyable. Instead of paying attention to the road for hours, you could read a book, or work on a hobby.
Where could this be implemented?
High speed rail could be implimented in many places around the world. Some countries around the world already use it! Japan and many parts of Europe already have advanced high speed rail networks. In Europe you can get to entirely different countries without having to drive at all, and more is planned. However, like others, high speed rail is an expensive solution and many poorer countries could not afford to impliment it without help.
To the side is a map of Europe's current high speed rail system.
So why arent we doing anything?
Funding from big oil and the car industry has kept America in a car dependant state for a long time. Our public transit is horrible compared to other countries and parking lots take up our land. High speed rail is not just about climate change, but is also the start of a big shift away from cars, and towards public transit.
